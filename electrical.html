Hiring an Electrician Guide

Why It Matters

Electrical work involves serious safety stakes. Improper work can cause fire hazards, code violations, and danger to your family. Always hire qualified professionals.

Licensing & Insurance

  • Understand journeyman vs. master electrician distinctions
  • Verify state licensing through official databases
  • Request proof of liability and workers' comp insurance

Scope of Work Considerations

  • Small repairs vs. panel upgrades vs. whole-house rewiring
  • Permit requirements vary by job type
  • Some work requires licensed master electricians

Evaluating Quotes

  • Request itemized estimates (labor, materials, permits)
  • Compare at least three bids
  • Be wary of quotes significantly lower than others

Safety & Code Compliance

  • Ensure NEC (National Electrical Code) adherence
  • Understand local inspection processes
  • Clarify who handles permit pulling

Warning Signs

  • Unlicensed "handyman" electrical work
  • Suggestions to skip permits to save money
  • Vague or verbal-only quotes
  • Reluctance to provide references

Questions to Ask

  • How do you handle unexpected issues mid-project?
  • What's included in your warranty?
  • Will the work pass inspection?
  • Who will be doing the actual work?

Tip: Always verify an electrician's license through Alabama's licensing board and ensure permits are pulled for any significant work.
