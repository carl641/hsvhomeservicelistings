Hiring Pest Control Guide

Why It Matters

Pest problems can affect your health, damage your property, and disrupt your peace of mind. Proper pest control requires the right approach for each situation.

Licensing & Certification

  • State pesticide applicator licensing
  • Company business license
  • Insurance requirements verification

Types of Services

  • One-time treatments vs. ongoing plans
  • General pest control vs. specialized (termites, bed bugs, wildlife)
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM) approaches

Evaluating Companies

  • Free inspections and assessments
  • Treatment methods and products used
  • Safety for children, pets, and plants

Understanding Contracts

  • Service frequency and coverage
  • What pests are covered
  • Re-treatment guarantees
  • Cancellation terms

Questions to Ask

  • What's causing the problem and how will you address it?
  • Are the products used EPA-registered?
  • What preparation is required from me?
  • What's the warranty period?

Red Flags

  • Scare tactics or pressure sales
  • Unwillingness to identify specific pests
  • No written service agreement
  • "Secret formula" claims

Tip: A reputable pest control company will identify the specific pest problem and explain their treatment approach clearly before beginning work.
