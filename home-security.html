Home Security Guide

Why It Matters

A security system protects your family and property, may reduce insurance costs, and provides peace of mind whether you're home or away.

Types of Security Systems

  • Professionally monitored vs. self-monitored
  • Wired vs. wireless systems
  • DIY installation vs. professional installation
  • Smart home integration capabilities

Core Components

  • Control panel and keypads
  • Door/window sensors and motion detectors
  • Security cameras and video doorbells
  • Smart locks and environmental sensors

Monitoring Services

  • 24/7 professional monitoring
  • Response protocols and dispatch procedures
  • Backup communication options
  • Mobile app capabilities

Evaluating Providers

  • Company reputation and longevity
  • Local vs. national companies
  • US-based monitoring centers
  • UL-listed monitoring certifications

Understanding Contracts

  • Contract length and early termination fees
  • Equipment ownership vs. leasing
  • What happens if you move
  • Price lock guarantees

Questions to Ask

  • What happens during power or internet outage?
  • How quickly do you contact authorities?
  • Can I take the system if I move?
  • Will this qualify for insurance discounts?

Smart Home Integration

  • Compatibility with Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit
  • Automation capabilities
  • Geofencing features

Red Flags

  • High-pressure door-to-door sales
  • Requests to see your current system
  • Extremely long contracts (5+ years)
  • Equipment that only works with their monitoring
  • Hidden fees revealed after signing

Tip: Ask about homeowner's insurance discounts - many insurers offer 5-20% off for monitored security systems.
