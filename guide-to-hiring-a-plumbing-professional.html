Guide to Hiring a Plumbing Professional

Everything Huntsville homeowners need to know about licensing, credentials, and vetting plumbers

Home › Plumbing › Hiring Guide

Executive Summary (TL;DR)

Selecting a plumbing contractor in Huntsville requires a strict verification of licensure through the Alabama Plumbers and Gas Fitters Examining Board (PGFB). The most critical distinction in this trade is between "Plumbing" and "Gas Fitting." These are separate certifications in Alabama. A licensed plumber generally cannot legally install or repair natural gas lines—a common necessity in Huntsville homes for water heaters and furnaces—unless they also hold a specific Gas Fitter certification. Homeowners must reject any contractor who claims a general "handyman" or "business" license is sufficient for plumbing work. Legally, only an apprentice registered with the state, a Journeyman, or a Master Plumber can perform the work, and an apprentice cannot work unsupervised. For vetting, the homeowner should demand to see the physical PGFB card, check the expiration date (renewals are annual), and verify that the individual holds the specific classification (Plumber vs. Gas Fitter) required for the job. Additionally, permits are mandatory for significant work in the City of Huntsville; homeowners should never pull permits on behalf of a contractor, as this transfers liability to the homeowner.

Deep Dive: Regulatory Framework and Vetting Protocol

The Hierarchy of Competence: Understanding PGFB Classifications

The trade of plumbing and gas fitting is governed by strict state statutes designed to prevent public health catastrophes, such as cross-connections that contaminate drinking water or gas leaks that cause explosions. The Alabama Plumbers and Gas Fitters Examining Board (PGFB) establishes a clear hierarchy of licensure that dictates who can perform work and under what conditions.1

1. The Apprentice

The entry-level designation is the Apprentice. In Alabama, an apprentice must be registered with the PGFB. Crucially, an apprentice is legally prohibited from working alone. They must be under the direct supervision of a licensed Journeyman or Master Plumber.1 A common cost-cutting tactic among unscrupulous companies is to send an apprentice to perform a repair unsupervised. This is a violation of state law. If a technician arrives at a Huntsville home, the homeowner has the right to ask for their card. If it says "Apprentice," a licensed supervisor must be present on-site.

2. The Journeyman

A Journeyman Plumber or Gas Fitter has demonstrated technical competence. To achieve this status, an individual must have completed two years as an apprentice or an approved apprenticeship program and passed a rigorous examination.1 The exam covers critical topics including materials, pipe sizing, venting, combustion air, and isometric reading.2 A Journeyman can perform work without direct supervision but generally cannot contract directly with the public as a business owner; they typically work as an employee of a Master Plumber.

3. The Master

The Master Plumber or Master Gas Fitter represents the highest level of licensure. In addition to proving technical mastery, a Master applicant must pass a "Business and Law Exam," ensuring they understand the legalities of contracting, lien laws, and business management.1 Every legitimate plumbing company in Huntsville must have a Master Plumber as the "responsible charge." This person puts their license on the line for the company's work.

The Gas Fitting Distinction: A Matter of Life and Safety

Alabama is unique in its strict separation of Plumbing and Gas Fitting. While the trades are related, they are not identical. A "Journeyman Plumber" is licensed to work on water and sewer pipes. A "Journeyman Gas Fitter" is licensed to work on natural gas and propane piping.1

Many home appliances, such as tankless water heaters, require both water and gas connections. A contractor holding only a plumbing license is legally barred from touching the gas piping. In Huntsville, where natural gas is prevalent, this is a frequent compliance issue. Homeowners installing gas logs, gas stoves, or gas water heaters must explicitly check for the "Gas Fitter" endorsement on the contractor's license card. The PGFB examination for gas fitters specifically tests on venting and combustion air requirements—critical knowledge to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning.1

Safety Alert: A contractor holding only a plumbing license is legally barred from touching gas piping. Always verify the "Gas Fitter" endorsement for any work involving natural gas or propane.

Reciprocity and Out-of-State Contractors

Huntsville's proximity to the Tennessee border often brings out-of-state contractors into the market. It is vital to note that the Alabama PGFB does not currently have active reciprocal agreements with other states.2 A plumber licensed in Tennessee cannot simply drive across the border and work in Madison County. They must apply to the Alabama board, prove their out-of-state license is in good standing, and potentially take the Alabama examination.3 If a contractor claims their Tennessee license "covers them" in Alabama, they are providing false information.

The Permitting Trap

The City of Huntsville and Madison County require permits for new installations, sewer line replacements, and water heater change-outs. The permit system creates a paper trail and triggers a municipal inspection to ensure code compliance.

Red Flag: A contractor who asks the homeowner to "pull the permit." In Huntsville, a homeowner can only pull a permit if they own the home, reside in it, and are performing the work themselves.4

The Risk: If a homeowner pulls the permit for a contractor, the homeowner effectively becomes the general contractor in the eyes of the law. If the worker is injured or the work is substandard, the homeowner assumes all liability. A licensed contractor in good standing will always pull their own permits.

Vetting Strategy: The Documentation Triad

To filter out illegitimate operators, the homeowner should request three specific documents before authorizing work:

  1. State License Card: Verify it is a current year card (renewals were due Dec 31st) and check for the "Gas Fitter" designation if applicable.5
  2. Municipal Privilege License: This proves the business is registered with the City of Huntsville or Madison County to pay taxes.6
  3. Certificate of Insurance: While the PGFB focuses on competency, the homeowner needs financial protection. A General Liability policy covers damages if a pipe bursts days after the plumber leaves.

Key Takeaways

  • Verify Specific Endorsements: Never assume a plumber can work on gas lines; demand to see the separate Gas Fitter license certification before authorizing work on heaters or stoves.
  • Enforce Supervision Rules: An Apprentice registered with the PGFB cannot legally work on a property without the physical presence of a Journeyman or Master.
  • Reject Out-of-State Licenses: Alabama has no reciprocity; a Tennessee or Georgia license is invalid in Huntsville unless the individual has obtained specific Alabama licensure.
  • Maintain Permit Integrity: Never obtain a building or plumbing permit on behalf of a contractor; this action shifts all liability for code compliance and safety to the homeowner.
