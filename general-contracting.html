Hiring a General Contractor Guide

Why It Matters

General contractors manage complex projects and coordinate multiple trades. Choosing the right one is essential for project success, budget control, and quality results.

Licensing & Credentials

  • State general contractor licensing
  • Insurance: general liability, workers' comp, builder's risk
  • Bonding requirements

Defining Project Scope

  • Detailed written scope of work
  • Plans and specifications
  • Allowances vs. fixed costs

Vetting Contractors

  • Years in business and project history
  • References from similar projects
  • Portfolio review and site visits
  • BBB and online reviews

Contracts & Payment

  • Fixed-price vs. cost-plus contracts
  • Payment schedules tied to milestones
  • Change order procedures
  • Lien waiver requirements

Communication & Management

  • Point of contact and availability
  • Progress updates and site meetings
  • Timeline and delay handling
  • Subcontractor management

Permits & Inspections

  • Who pulls permits
  • Inspection scheduling
  • Final sign-offs and certificate of occupancy

Red Flags

  • Large deposits upfront (10-15% is typical)
  • No written contract or vague terms
  • Reluctance to provide references
  • Not pulling required permits
  • Price significantly below other bids

Dispute Resolution

  • Warranty terms
  • Mediation and arbitration clauses
  • Mechanic's lien protections

Tip: Never pay more than 10-15% upfront. Tie payments to completed milestones and always get lien waivers from subcontractors.
