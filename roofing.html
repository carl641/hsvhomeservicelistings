Hiring a Roofer Guide

Why It Matters

Your roof protects your largest investment. Poor roofing work can lead to water damage, insurance issues, and costly repairs down the road.

Licensing & Insurance (Critical)

  • Verify liability insurance minimums
  • Confirm workers' compensation (you can be liable without it)
  • Check state licensing where required

Types of Roofing Projects

  • Repairs vs. overlay vs. full tear-off
  • Material options: asphalt, metal, tile, slate

Evaluating Contractors

  • Local presence and reputation (beware storm chasers)
  • Manufacturer certifications (GAF, CertainTeed, Owens Corning)
  • Request portfolio of completed work

Understanding Quotes

  • Material specifications and grades
  • Underlayment and flashing details
  • Ventilation assessment
  • Cleanup and disposal included

Warranties

  • Manufacturer warranty vs. workmanship warranty
  • Warranty transferability for resale

Insurance Claims

  • Work with your insurer directly
  • Avoid contractors who "handle everything"
  • Document everything thoroughly

Red Flags

  • Door-to-door solicitation after storms
  • Requests for large upfront deposits
  • No physical business address
  • Pressure to sign immediately

Tip: Always verify a roofer is local with an established presence. Storm chasers often disappear before warranty issues arise.
